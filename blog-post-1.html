The $2,028 Cart Recovery Playbook

How We Recovered Lost Revenue for a Clothing Brand in 14 Days

Caleb van Rooyen
· 8 min read · March 2026

Most clothing brand owners assume cart abandonment is just a cost of doing business online. Somebody fills their basket, gets distracted, and disappears — and that's that. You've already paid to get them there. You're not getting them back.

We disagree. And the $2,028 sitting in this brand's Shopify dashboard — generated in 14 days without a single paid ad — makes our case better than we ever could.

The Setup

Velour Fits is a US-based clothing brand running on Shopify. They had a real product people liked, decent organic traffic from Instagram, and a loyal base of past buyers. What they didn't have was any automation. No cart recovery sequence. No DM follow-up system. No past-buyer reactivation. Every sale was manual. Every abandoned cart was lost forever.

When we first audited their store, the numbers were familiar. Roughly 68% of carts were being abandoned — close to the industry average. Their conversion rate was sitting below 1.5%. And they had a list of past buyers who hadn't been contacted in months. Thousands of dollars in recoverable revenue, sitting completely untouched.

The store wasn't broken. It just had no system keeping money from walking out the door.

What We Built

We deployed the Nextstep AI core system: a 3-step cart recovery sequence powered by n8n and GPT-4o, an Instagram DM automation layer, and a buyer reactivation flow for the cold past-buyer list.

The whole stack was live in under 7 days. Here's what each piece does:

  • Cart abandonment recovery: A 3-email AI-personalised sequence fires automatically every time someone leaves without buying. The emails reference the exact products they left behind.
  • Instagram DM automation: Every DM that comes in gets an intelligent, personalised reply — qualified, answered, and directed toward checkout without manual input.
  • Buyer reactivation flow: Past buyers who hadn't purchased in 60+ days received a re-engagement sequence built around their previous purchase history.
  • Purchase gate: The moment someone converts, all active sequences stop immediately — no awkward follow-ups after the sale is done.
$0
Paid ad spend during the 14-day window. Every dollar recovered came from the automation system alone.

How It Works Step by Step

The cart recovery system is the engine. Here's the exact sequence we deployed:

T
Trigger: Cart Abandoned
Shopify fires a webhook the moment a cart is abandoned. n8n picks it up, pulls the cart contents, and kicks off the sequence.
Immediately
1
Email 1 — "You left something behind"
GPT-4o writes a short, warm reminder email personalised to the exact items in the cart. No generic templates. No awkward copy.
1 hour after abandonment
2
Email 2 — Social proof + urgency
The second email introduces a touch of urgency around stock levels and includes social proof relevant to the product category. Still personalised by AI.
24 hours after abandonment
3
Email 3 — Final nudge
One final, low-pressure email. This is the last touchpoint before the sequence closes. Often where the conversion happens for high-intent buyers who just needed time.
48 hours after abandonment
✓
Purchase Gate — Sequence stops
The moment a purchase is confirmed, n8n cancels all pending emails for that shopper. No follow-ups after conversion. Clean, professional, no awkward double-sends.

Can This Work for Your Brand?

This system works for clothing brands that have real traffic and real customers — even if those numbers aren't huge. You don't need 10,000 monthly visitors to see results. Velour Fits was a small brand with organic Instagram traffic and a modest customer list.

The system works when:

  • Your brand has a functional Shopify store with email capture at checkout
  • You're getting at least some traffic — even 500–1,000 monthly sessions is enough
  • You have a list of past buyers (even a small one) who haven't been reactivated
  • You want automation running within 7 days — not 7 weeks
